| DAY 2: Aug. 24 (MON) | SEOUL |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |
| Enjoy a full-day tour of Seoul, beginning with a visit to Gyeongbokgung Palace, the first and largest of the city’s five grand palaces. Witness the colorful Royal Guard Changing Ceremony, then explore the National Folk Museum to gain insight into traditional Korean life, customs, and culture. Continue to Bukchon Hanok Village, home to over 900 beautifully preserved traditional Korean houses (hanok), offering a glimpse into Seoul’s past amid a modern cityscape. Next, visit Gwangjang Market, one of Korea’s oldest and most vibrant traditional markets. Famous for its lively atmosphere and authentic street food, the market is an ideal place to experience local flavors while observing everyday Seoul life.
Before returning to the hotel, stop by Cheonggyecheon Stream, a restored urban waterway that provides a peaceful setting for relaxation, scenic walks, and cultural experiences in the heart of the city. | DAY 2: Aug. 24 (MON) | SEOUL |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |
| Enjoy a full-day tour of Seoul, beginning with a visit to Gyeongbokgung Palace, the first and largest of the city’s five grand palaces. Witness the colorful Royal Guard Changing Ceremony, then explore the National Folk Museum to gain insight into traditional Korean life, customs, and culture. Continue to Bukchon Hanok Village, home to over 900 beautifully preserved traditional Korean houses (hanok), offering a glimpse into Seoul’s past amid a modern cityscape. Next, visit Gwangjang Market, one of Korea’s oldest and most vibrant traditional markets. Famous for its lively atmosphere and authentic street food, the market is an ideal place to experience local flavors while observing everyday Seoul life.
Before returning to the hotel, stop by Cheonggyecheon Stream, a restored urban waterway that provides a peaceful setting for relaxation, scenic walks, and cultural experiences in the heart of the city.
