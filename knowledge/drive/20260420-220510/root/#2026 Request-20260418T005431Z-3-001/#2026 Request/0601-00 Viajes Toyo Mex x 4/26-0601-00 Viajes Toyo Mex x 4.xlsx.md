| Drive past the former Presidential House, Cheongwadae. Then, visit Gyeongbokgung Palace, the largest of five palaces, and watch the guard changing ceremony. Continue on to Insadong Street, filled with antiques and traditional crafts & goods. Then explore Gwangjang Market, which offers a vibrant mix of traditional Korean street food and bustling stalls. | DAY 3: June 03 (WED)  SEOUL - GYEONGJU - BUSAN |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |
| Check out from the hotel and meet your Spanish speaking guide at the lobby of the hotel. Then transfer to Seoul Station and take the KTX (Express Train) to Gyeongju, a city known as "Museum Without Walls". Upon arrival, visit Bulguksa Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Then, proceed to Seokguram Grotto, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Next, take a step back in time at Gyochon Traditional Village, a historic hanok chock full of live demonstrations, cute cafes and courtyards plus one of the best places to pose in hanbok dress. Visit Tumuli Park and Cheonmachong tomb, home to the tombs of the royal and nobles. Transfer to Busan and rest at leisure. |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |

| Start the day with a visit to Haedong Yonggungsa Temple, a rare temple situated on the coast. Then, stroll through Jagalchi Fish Market, Korea's largest seafood market. Continue to the bustling Gukje Market, where local street food and eclectic goods provide a rich taste of Busan's everyday life. Next, head to Gamcheon Cultural Village, where narrow alleyways wind through brightly painted houses adorned with murals and art installations.
