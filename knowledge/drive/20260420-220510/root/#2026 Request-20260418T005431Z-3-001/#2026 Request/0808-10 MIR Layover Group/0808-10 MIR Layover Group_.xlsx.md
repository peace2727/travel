| DAY 3: August 9 (Sun) | Seoul - Optional City Tour |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |
| Our journey begins from Gyeongbokgung Palace — the grand royal palace of the Joseon Dynasty. Here, you’ll witness the Change of the Royal Guard Ceremony, a colorful and rhythmic reenactment that brings Korea’s royal past vividly to life.
Next, we’ll visit Namsangol Hanok Village, a peaceful collection of traditional Korean houses nestled at the foot of Namsan Mountain. It’s a quiet escape that shows how people once lived centuries ago — simple, elegant, and in harmony with nature.
Now, it’s time for lunch, and what could be better than bulgogi, Korea’s signature marinated beef dish. 
After lunch, we’ll dive into the vibrant energy of Gwangjang Market, one of Seoul’s oldest. The scent of crispy bindaetteok and fresh gimbap fills the air, and every corner bursts with local color.
Then, we’ll stroll through Insadong Street, where art galleries, antique shops, and teahouses create a perfect blend of old and new. Finally, we’ll unwind along the Cheonggyecheon Stream, a serene oasis running through the heart of the city — a beautiful symbol of how modern Seoul cherishes its past while flowing gracefully into the future. |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |  |
